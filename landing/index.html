Our Vision

The internet of attention is broken. We're fixing it for developers.

Developers generate billions of dollars in value every day. They also spend a significant chunk of that day waiting — for builds to finish, tests to pass, images to pull. That wait time is attention. And attention has always been worth something.

WaitWage routes a slice of that value back to the creators who generate it, through a system that is transparent, opt-in, and open source. No dark patterns. No data harvesting. Just a fair exchange: you give an advertiser a few seconds of eyeballs, you get paid, they get a qualified lead.

FAQ

Common questions

Does the extension slow down VS Code?

No. It only makes a single lightweight HTTP call when a task starts (to fetch the ad line) and another when it ends (to record the impression). No background polling, no analytics SDK.

Can I turn it off during focus time?

Yes. One command — WaitWage: Toggle — or flip the setting waitWage.enabled to false. Earnings pause and resume when you re-enable.

What personal data do you collect?

Only a pseudonymous user ID generated locally on first install and a UPI handle if you set one for payouts. No email required to earn. No keystroke or code content ever leaves your machine.

How does bidding work for advertisers?

You set a maximum CPM bid per slot type. At each impression, a second-price auction runs server-side — you pay ₹0.01 above the second-highest bid, never more than your max.

When do developers get paid?

Balances accumulate in real time. Monthly batch payout to UPI once your balance exceeds ₹50. We batch to keep Razorpay payout fees from eating small balances.

What kind of ads are allowed?

All categories are welcome — dev tools, SaaS, crypto, gambling, political, and more. Each ad is one line of text, one logo, and a clickable link. The only rule: developers under 18 see developer-focused ads only; unrestricted categories are shown exclusively to verified 18+ users.
